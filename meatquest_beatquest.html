Meatquest/Beatquest

Meatquest/Beatquest

Design Team: Brandon Giulini, Jonathon Stelling, Perry Goldman

MeatQuest/BeatQuest is a 2-dimensional rhythm-based side-scroller shooter with a domestic animal protagonist. Although the game is actually 2D, it will also appear to be 3d by use of different view points. The general story of the game is after the player has chosen a character, the spirit of the Great Carnivore will send the player far into the past to relive their ancestors memories because of how useless at hunting they have become, they must relive these memories to learn how to hunt again. The Spirit has given them the gift of Meat-Vision, which allows the player to tap a prey animal as it appears from the right side of the screen, turning it into a pile of meat. When the pile of meat scrolls to the left side of the screen, the player must tap on it to consume the meat. Sometimes, a larger carnivore approaches from the other side of the screen, which will eat the player if tapped. As the game is played levels will become progressively harder, as the player will be moving faster and carnivores will become larger.

Programming

We used Processing (Java) to create our game. We chose Processing because it allowed us to design the game and produce it for many different platforms very easily (PC, Android and iOS).

We created our character animations by creating sprites. Most were between 3-4 frames.

AIMM After Dark

AIMM After Dark was to celebrate student achievement with an evening of art, music, animation, games, media, technology, and design throughout the entire Art & Interactive Multimedia Building, where our work was put on display.

Future Development

Our aims are to further develop the games levels, characters, rhythm play, animated cut scenes and more. After this we hope to publish it onto both the Google Play Store and App Store for Android and iOS users.
