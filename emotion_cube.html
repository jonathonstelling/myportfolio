Emotion Cube

Emotion Cube

Design Team: Jonathon Stelling, Aidan Harris, Elliot Quinn, Rob Miller & Tim Tetlow

In interaction folly our group had to come up with a product which lacked sense, function or usefulness, but it had to remain interesting. Our idea was a cube which had a button placed on the top that the user needed to press to deactivate the device, but whenever the user would get close the cube would roll away from them.

Concepts

We began by coming up with how our cube would roll. We decided that sqaure wheels would be the best solution as they would be a folly too, compared to just using circular wheels.

After we had decided to use square wheels we then went forward and begin calculations to find out how powerful our motor needed to be and if our solution was possible.

Final Design

In our final design we chose to make the cube transparent as we have incorporated LED’s inside to become active as the cube moved. The cube features 2 individual motors to rotate the sqaure wheels located on either side of the cube, it also features 3 ultrasonic sensors, located on the back, top and front of the cube. The ultrasonic sensors were used to pick up any human contact coming within 200mm of the cube. If the ultrasonic sensors detected any human contact they wiould then transmit this information to the processor, to then activate the motors and LED’s.

Prototype

We then began to make a prototype of our final design. By using PICAXE chip (PICAXE-18M2 microcontroller) and a suitable motor driving chip we were then able to create a working prototype. We decided to use wood for our sqaure wheel in our first prototype, as they would withstand the forces subjected on them better compared to acrylic sheets.
