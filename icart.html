Jonathon Stelling | Product Design Engineer

iCart

Date Completed: 2000

We worked with a company called ADM Automation Limited, located in Gateshead, United Kingdom. Our design challenge was to design a trolley which would work with the iCart in moving a box from one part of a warehouse/production line to the other, the only constraint was that the trolley couldn’t contain any electrical mechanisms.

Concepts

We then started to think of different ways we could move a box on and off the trolley by using no electrical components. Some ideas included using the force from the wheels or from pushing into the bench, to using the weight from one box to cause another box to be pushed from the trolley.

First Design

We quickly eliminated mechanisms involving the trolley wheels and the weight of the boxes as they were unrealiable solutions, simply because the weight of the boxes could vary and the mechanism connected to the wheel would increase friction. Our final design used a push system. As the trolley aproached the worktop and the underside bar came into contact with the table, the bar was then pushed back pulling the connecting rods with it, which inturn rotated the arms, pushing a box onto the table.

Prototype

Once we prototyped our design, we then began to test it. After testing we found that the design was too complex and wasn’t reliable enough to be used in operations. We then went back to develop our design further, to try to simplify the mechanism.

Final Design

After developing our design we managed to simplify the mechanishm on our trolley, reducing the amount of moving parts and also increasing the reliablilty of the trolley. To add more functionalilty to our trolley we decided to make trolleys modular, so that they could attach to each other, so they could load and unload more boxes within the same operation.
