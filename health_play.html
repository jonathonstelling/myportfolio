Health Play

Design Team:

Jonathon Stelling

Alex Vescio

Elliot Quinn

Joe Norton

Rob Miller

Tim Tetlow

Date Completed: 2015

Our brief was to design a toy which would improve a childs condition who is suffering from Psoriasis, a chronic and acute condition of the skin, and the relationship they have with the hospital, so that both parents and healthcare professionals can monitor the patients behaviour in relation to their condition and the toy.

Concept

The platform for our concepts was that it would contain a hub and a module. The hub would be left in the childs bedroom and would contain treatments, while the module would be carried with the child. The concept we took forward was using a popular theme of superheros which appeals to children. The superhero would serve as the module unit and would have a 3D scan of the childs head as the superhero’s head, which would give the child voice reminders to apply treatment. The hub which would be the superhero’s lair where their treatments would be contained, such as cream and a humidifier.

Final Design

Prototype

With the use of a 3D printer, laser cutter and other manfuacturing methods we were able to create a working prototype. We used Arduino boards to create the working electronic side of the prototype, it was used to control the LCD display, alarm, humidifier and the speakers

App

Our app will work alongside our product to help inform parents of their childs condition and their progress with their treatment, telling them whether or not their child has been applying their cream. It will also provide parents with reminders on times and dates of hospital appointments.
